# Aurora character consistency guide

Status: owner-supplied visual identity; production asset selection and license
record pending

This guide records Aurora's repeatable visual markers without copying the
private source illustrations into the repository. It applies to launcher
icons, wallpapers, screenshots, promotional art, and any future generated
derivative.

## Identity invariants

- Aurora is an adult anime-styled woman with violet eyes and long, near-black
  hair that may catch blue or violet rim light.
- Her silhouette uses one high side ponytail. Do not substitute twin tails,
  buns, a low ponytail, loose-only hair, cat ears, or horns.
- Exactly two short violet hairpins sit parallel on the loose front bangs,
  opposite the ponytail. They must remain separate, aligned, and visible.
- A distinct violet, angular capital-`A` clip sits at the ponytail base. It is
  not a third bangs pin and must not drift onto the jacket or background.
- In the standard three-quarter composition, the bangs pins appear on the
  viewer's left and the ponytail plus `A` clip appear on the viewer's right. A
  mirrored composition is acceptable only when all of those side cues mirror
  together.
- Her core outfit is a black hooded technical jacket with restrained violet
  hardware or edge light. A small angular Aurora mark on the sleeve is
  permitted; extra words and unrelated logos are not.
- Her normal expression is calm, focused, or quietly confident. Avoid
  childlike proportions and exaggerated mascot features unless a separately
  approved chibi variant is requested.

Accessories such as a penguin charm, mug, earrings, bag, or computer belong to
specific scenes. They are optional and must never replace the core hair and
eye markers.

## AuroraSMS icon language

- Combine Aurora with one unmistakable SMS speech-bubble silhouette.
- Use a deep navy or near-black field with violet, magenta, and cyan aurora
  light. The aurora may form the bubble outline, halo, or restrained fill.
- Keep the face, both bangs pins, violet eyes, ponytail base, and `A` clip
  inside the adaptive icon's central safe zone.
- Prefer a bold silhouette and limited background detail that remain readable
  at 48 px. Stars and glow are supporting texture, not the primary mark.
- Do not add the app name, stray letters, a watermark, or the AuroraCamera
  icon. The approved `A` hair clip and optional jacket mark are the only letter
  forms in the character art.
- The final adaptive icon requires separate foreground, background, and simple
  monochrome layers; a flattened concept preview is not a production asset.

## Review checklist

Before accepting any Aurora render, verify all of the following at full size
and at launcher-thumbnail size:

1. One high side ponytail is present.
2. Two parallel violet bangs pins are present and not fused or misplaced.
3. The violet angular `A` clip is attached to the ponytail base.
4. Eyes are violet; hair is near-black; the technical jacket is black.
5. No unrequested ears, horns, hair ornaments, text, or logos appeared.
6. All side-specific markers mirror together if the composition is mirrored.
7. For AuroraSMS icons, the speech bubble is immediately recognizable.
8. Cropping and launcher masks do not remove any core identity marker.

## Provenance boundary

The defining details above were derived from private, owner-supplied visual
references. Those source images remain outside the public repository and APK.
Generated concept previews remain selection-only until the owner chooses one
and the artwork permission/provenance record required by
